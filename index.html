About

Photo by Jungu Kang

Hello! My name is Ruoqing Yao (姚若轻). I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Linguistics Department at UC Santa Cruz. Before joining UCSC, I received my B.S. in Computer Science and Linguistics from William & Mary. I am originally from Beijing, China.

Research

I am primarily a psycholinguist. My main research interest lies at the intersection of ambiguity resolution and pronoun resolution. I currently investigate the processing mechanisms of pronominal ambiguity. I am interested in the boundaries among ambiguity advantage, disadvantage, and underspecification. My advisor is Matt Wagers.
